  1. A bank manager wants to know the mean amount of mortgage paid per month by homeowners in an area. A random sample of 120 homeowners selected from this area showed that they pay an average of $1575 per month for their mortgages. The population standard deviation of all such mortgages is $215. Assume that the mortgages amounts follow a normal distribution.

    1. (3) Find a 97% confidence interval for the mean amount of mortgage paid per month by all homeowners in this area.
    2. (2) Suppose that the manager wants to estimate the mean amount of mortgage paid per month to within $50 at 97% confidence. How many homeowners should they select?
    3. (2) In another area, the mean amount is $1650. Based on your answer in a, is there reason to believe the mortgage amounts are different in this area?

  4. A random sample of 34 participants in a Zumba dance class had their heart rates measured before and after a moderate 10-minute Zumba workout. The sample mean and standard deviation of the increase in heart rate are 51.676 and 9.204 respectively.

    1. (4) Calculate a 98% lower bound for the mean increase in heart rate. Interpret.
    2. (2) Calculate a 95% two sided confidence interval for the mean increase in heart rate.
    3. (4) Calculate a 95% prediction interval for the increase in heart rate for a randomly selected person who takes a Zumba class in the future. Interpret.

Calculate a 97% confidence interval for the percentage of all such companies that provide such facilities on site. Interpret.
